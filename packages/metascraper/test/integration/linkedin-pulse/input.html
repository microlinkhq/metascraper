    How Can AI Change Financial Services? Consolidation of the #FinAI tweetstorm

    May 26, 2016
    • 23 views
    • 4 Likes
    • 0 Comments
    • Share on LinkedIn
    • Share on Facebook
    • Share on Twitter

    We had a tweetstorm today on #FinAI and the role AI will play to change financial services. Here is the consolidation of tweets. 

    1/ Fintech in 2016 is similar to the Internet in 1996 – Yahoo, Netscape and Excite had arrived, and the startup ecosystem is abuzz.

    2/ Fintech is still missing its equivalent of Google, Facebook and Amazon. This will change dramatically over the next 10 years.

    3/ Banks today are in watch-and-see mode <> Similar to Microsoft, IBM, in 1996.

    4/ In 1996 – Users of technology interacted with software, that worked on an OS, that ran on a machine. (highly simplified version).

    5/ Today, hardware is moving to the cloud. Software is eating the world, and that in turn is being eaten by AI. (thanks @pmarca)

    6/ Financial Services in 2025-2030 will look very different. To understand how, let’s start with how it works today.

    7/ In 2016 – Financial services users interact with people (brokers, advisors), that distribute products on a financial platform.

    8/ Financial Platform is referred to as Origination - the backbone for all financial products (balance sheet, capital, etc.).

    9/Origination is still almost 100% done by incumbent banks. Even in Fintech - #robos use #Apex, #Pershing, P2P lending use #webbank.

    10/ Origination is heavily regulated with liquidity and capital requirements to protect clients and balance system-wide risks

    11/Origination is to the financial industry what hardware is to tech - it powers everything.

    12/ Distribution takes economic payoffs or assets, packages them into financial products, and sells these products to end clients.

    13/ Distribution is how banks generate profit – transacting in products at cheaper than fair-value or for a commission/ profit share.

    14/ Distribution is regulated by laws on fair disclosure, fiduciary duty, conflicts of interests, and product characteristics.

    15/ Distribution is what Software is to tech - is the economic interaction between clients and Origination via financial products

    16/Today, “financial products are sold, not bought” - Human Capital powers this push vs. pull model via branch office/sales call.

    17/ While electronic trading/online banking have reduced our branch visits, any intellectual decision making is done by human capital

    18/ Human Capital makes sure both origination and distribution works in line with markets, laws, and client needs.

    19/ Human capital is to the financial industry what UX is to tech - the UX of interacting with clients and providing expert advice

    20/ Human capital is also what OS is to tech - making sure Origination and Distribution works right to laws and obligations.

    21/ Origination, distribution, and human capital are vertically integrated at banks, hence easier to offer many types of products.

    22/ Economies of scale are a strong barrier to entry for #Fintech. Hence Fintech’s focus on faster technology or cheaper price.

    23/ Human capital at banks are a fixed cost and can’t provide personalization to all - but consumers want personalized solutions

    24/ To change finance services, we need to change the role of human capital and focus on scaling personalized solutions

    25/ Prediction 1 - By 202X, much of Human capital, will be replaced by “FinAI”. While origination/ Distribution will be on a “FinCloud”

    26/ Why #FinAI? Because AI has been proven to handle mass amounts of changing bigdata, high dimensionality, and can learn over time.

    27/ #FinAI is adaptive learning and personalized advice (think: complex recommendation engines) for your finances.

    28/ Human experts calibrate #FinAI - but it remains unbiased and transparent, assisted by the move to the #FinCloud.

    29/ #FinCloud is the new class of entities that fulfills all the infrastructure needs of creating and distributing products per #FinAI

    30/ In #FinCloud distribution is not product based, it provides “bricks” or unbundled components - to be bundled as per #FinAI logic.

    31/Based on available #FinCloud bricks, #FinAI can decide how to build a personalized solution vs. matching / filtering available products.

    32/ With #FinCloud bricks, you don’t need 100,000 almost 99% similar mutual funds, 90% of which underperform their benchmarks.

    33/ In #FinCloud origination is agnostic to distribution, vs. vertically integrated at banks. Need more #Apex #Webbank #Pershing for #FinAI

    34/ Existing banks will need adapt as #fintech supplies an increasing portion of #FinCloud. #FinAI is agnostic to #FinCloud provider

    35/ Prediction 2 - #FinAI will be the panacea by which personalized financial advice will scale to a mass-market

    36/For #FinAI to scale consistently and securely we also need #FinProfile and #FinIdentity.

    37/#FinProfile is your financial data, your demographic profile, and your future plans / priorities - connected and centralized for #FinAI

    38/ For #FinProfile to become a standard, regulators need to ensure consumers (not banks) own all their financial data to link it to #FinAI

    39/ To ensure privacy on #FinAI, we also need unique #FinIdentity, ideally based on universal biometrics - like #aadhar #id2020 initiatives

    40/ Universal #FinIdentity makes account opening/ closing in a #FinCloud very easy. It is the future and important to #IoT and #FinAI

    41/ So now we have #FinProfile, #FinIdentity, #FinCloud. Let’s see how #FinAI brings them together.

    42/ #FinIdentity will be used by #FinAI to summon your #FinProfile securely

    43/ #FinAI uses #FinProfile to understand your life-profile - how your assets, debt, cashflows evolve over time with your plans/priorities.

    44/ With real-time, accurate updates to your #FinProfile and markets, #FinAI provides real-time information and timely advice on when to act

    45/Real time interactive feedback on your finances and market conditions via #FinAI keeps your aware and engaged

    46/ #FinAI also uses #FinProfile to scan and personalize the best investment and banking “bricks” for you from the #FinCloud

    47/ With 500 bricks on the #FinCloud, each relatively uncorrelated to the other, #FinAI can personalize from billions of combinations.

    48/#FinAI will ignore fake bricks with higher fees or similar beta. Diversification in bricks adds value, replication does not.

    49/ Let’s explore some of the ways #FinAI can work.

    50/ #FinAI for Cash Management - balancing between cash + investing and ensuring your cash is held at highest deposit rates offered.

    51/ #FinAI knows your plans and how your liquidity needs vary over time as a result

    52/ #FinAI projects liquidity and finds cash you’ll need on hand and puts that into highest deposit rates offered. Rate sensing AI.

    53/Each brick in the #FinCloud will provide the #FinAI amount of deposits it’s seeking, the rate, and the term.

    54/Excess cash is invested by #FinAI that personalize alpha/beta investments based on your life-profile’s liquidity and risk needs.

    55/#FinAI can monitor and if appropriate, propose direct investing in more complex products like peer to peer lending.

    56/#FinAI for funding a new home, kids’ college - Lending by #FinAI uses your #FinProfile, not just FICO scores. More accurate.

    57/Credit could be direct from seller to lender, in-real time, managed by #FinAI enabled by #FinProfile and stored on the #FinCloud

    58/ In a #FinAI world regulators must apply fiduciary and suitability standards, just like they would with human capital

    59/ Regulatory/infrastructure complexity reduces as we can stop regulating bundled products and regulate the bricks and the #FinAI instead

    60/ Regulators must allow #FinAI to interface directly with clients. No need for human advisors in the middle, if the process is automated.

    61/#FinAI does not discriminate on sex, race, gender, national origin, even income or wealth.

    62/ #FinAI makes services affordable, personalized, and 24/7 - adapting as your plans and markets shift. Human capital cannot do this.

    63/Decrease in human capital, replaced by AI, means lower prices for financial services. #FinAI enables personalization for a mass-market.

    64/ Prediction 3 - #FinAI with the best models and integrity will be the winner. #FinAI is a winner takes all market.

    65/ #FinCloud will increasingly become a #utility as #FinAI grows in strength. Wallet-share of client fees will go to #FinAI.

    66/ The brand of the #FinCloud will matter much less than the brand, quality, integrity of the #FinAI

    67/ Regulation and hard rules will focus on ensuring the #FinAI is unbiased, accurate, and is acting as a fiduciary.

    68/ #FinAI levels the playing field between financial expert and novice by making expert advice personalized for anyone.

    69/#FinAI will be the epicenter of innovations in financial markets. We are still in the very early stages of #FinAI.

    70/ What do you think? Tweet on how you would use #FinAI to finding new ways to solve problems and improve financial services
