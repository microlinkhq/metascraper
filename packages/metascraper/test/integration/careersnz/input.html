IT isn’t only for the super-technical

In the second article of our series on information and communications technology (ICT), we’re going to look at the different kinds of jobs in the industry.

When you hear "ICT" or "IT" (information technology), you probably think of people who write computer code for a living, or who help run and fix computer systems. And you probably think it’s just for nerdy guys who’re into gaming and the indoors.

There’s much more to it than that, though! There’s a huge range of jobs and people in the industry. Below, we list a few examples of these jobs – demand for all of them is growing.

Artists

If you’re artistic, have no fear – there’s space for you in IT. As the gaming industry continues to grow, for example, there’s an increasing need for artists to work on the visual elements of games, from storyboards to scenery to characters, and more.

  • Read about how artists work in gaming

Web and graphic designers

Into design? In IT, graphic designers work to make beautiful visuals like icons, logos and controls for websites and apps. They also ensure the right typography and great colour schemes are chosen.

  • Find out more about graphic design

If you like design and are interested in the technical side of things, you can also train to be a web designer. Web designers help to build or redesign websites. They work on how websites look and behave in different browsers, and need to have good knowledge of web languages like HTML and CSS.

Some graphic designers are also web designers.

  • Read more about working as a web designer
  • Find out more about web design

UX and UI specialists

UX stands for user experience, and UI for user interface. People who work in UX and UI study how people interact with websites, apps and more, and find ways to improve people’s experiences. If you love understanding people and helping make sure they have a great time using something, then UX might be for you!

As digital products and services increasingly become part of our daily lives, people who know how to make those products and services simple and enjoyable to use are also in more demand.

  • Find out more about different design jobs in UX and UI

Writers and editors (web and technical)

Do you love language? Does getting a story just right make you happy? Then you might want to be a writer or editor, and create web content. This can range from helping organisations write/edit the content for their website, to helping people who make online videos get their interview or script right.

Content design – a relatively new field – mixes writing in plain English with an understanding of how people interact with and understand information. Governments in countries such as New Zealand, Australia and the UK are hiring content designers to work on their websites and services.

  • Find out more about what content designers do
  • Check out the Govt.nz website

If you love detail, you may want to consider becoming a technical writer. Technical writers write documentation, such as manuals, that help people use a product or service.

  • Read more about technical writing

Data scientists

Love data? Or finding patterns? Data science is all about that. Data scientists – sometimes called business intelligence consultants or analysts – look at large amounts of data for patterns to help an organisation work better and find new competitive edges. The field is growing too, as humanity produces more data and organisations globally start looking at big data.

  • Find out why data scientists are in demand
  • Check out these interviews with data scientists

IT business analysts

IT business analysts liaise between the business and IT departments of an organisation. They help organisations figure out:

  • what their true business problem is (and how to solve it) before choosing a technology solution
  • how to apply new systems and processes, and get people using them.

IT business analysts are often involved in developing websites and apps, and help to prioritise changes, improvements or new features.

To sum up? You don’t need to be the nerdiest person in the room to get involved in IT. And women can do technical work just as well as men. The more different kinds of people in the industry, the better products and services it can provide for us all.
