Email security startup Agari raised $22 million in funding on Tuesday to put a stop to phishing attacks on business emails.

Norwest Venture Partners led the Series D round with participation from all existing investors including Alloy Ventures, Battery Ventures, First Round Capital, Greylock Partners and Scale Venture Partners. The company has raised $44.7 million to date.

CEO Patrick Peterson founded the San Mateo-based startup in 2009 and built a platform designed to halt targeted phishing attacks by detecting imposter emails. Prior to Agari, Peterson served as vice president of technology at IronPort and was a fellow at Cisco Systems.

The company claims its platform analyzes more than 10 billion messages per day and has prevented 3.5 billion fraudulent emails from being delivered in 2015. The company's customers include six of the worlds top 10 banks and five of the world's leading social media networks.

"Over the past several years, the cybersecurity market has grown exponentially with new companies and products being launched every few months," Peterson said in a press release. "Enterprises are clamoring for solutions to stop targeted email attacks to protect their corporate assets, as well as avoid the humiliating publicity that comes with these attacks.

According to the Federal Bureau of Investigation, business email compromise incidents rose by 270 percent and amounted to more than $2.3 billion in losses in less than two years.

The company will use the new funds to expand development and deployment activities.
